# Headline Standards

## Why Headlines Matter

- 80% of readers only see the headline
- Headlines determine click-through rates
- Search engines weight title heavily
- Social shares depend on headline appeal

**Rule**: Write at least 25 headline variations before selecting your final title.

---

## Headline Specifications

### Length
- **Ideal**: 6-13 words
- **Character limit**: 50-60 characters (search display)
- **Social**: Under 70 characters for full display

### Structure
- Front-load keywords when natural
- Lead with benefit or curiosity
- Avoid clickbait that content won't deliver

---

## Proven Headline Formulas

### 1. How-To
**Formula**: How to [Achieve Desired Outcome]

Examples:
- How to Write Headlines That Get Clicks
- How to Double Your Email Open Rates
- How to Start a Blog in 30 Minutes

**When to use**: Educational content, tutorials, skill-building

---

### 2. Numbered List
**Formula**: [Number] [Adjective] Ways to [Achieve Outcome]

Examples:
- 7 Proven Ways to Increase Conversion Rates
- 21 Email Subject Lines That Actually Work
- 5 Simple Habits of Highly Productive Writers

**Power numbers**: 5, 7, 10, 21 (odd numbers often outperform)

**When to use**: Listicles, roundups, tips posts

---

### 3. Question
**Formula**: [Question Your Audience is Asking]?

Examples:
- What's the Best Time to Post on Instagram?
- Why Isn't Your Content Getting Traffic?
- Are You Making These SEO Mistakes?

**When to use**: Addressing common questions, problem content

---

### 4. Curiosity Gap
**Formula**: [Intriguing Statement] + [Implied Secret/Unknown]

Examples:
- The Content Strategy Most Marketers Ignore
- What Nobody Tells You About Going Viral
- The Real Reason Your Blog Posts Flop

**When to use**: Thought leadership, contrarian takes

---

### 5. Direct Benefit
**Formula**: [Get/Achieve] [Specific Benefit] [Timeframe if applicable]

Examples:
- Get More Traffic With Less Content
- Double Your Leads in 30 Days
- Build a 6-Figure Business From Your Blog

**When to use**: Promotional content, bottom-funnel pieces

---

### 6. "Ultimate/Complete" Guide
**Formula**: The [Ultimate/Complete/Definitive] Guide to [Topic]

Examples:
- The Complete Guide to Content Marketing in 2024
- The Ultimate SEO Checklist for New Websites
- The Definitive Guide to Email Automation

**When to use**: Comprehensive resources, pillar content

---

### 7. Mistake/Problem
**Formula**: [Number] [Topic] Mistakes [Costing You/Killing Your] [Outcome]

Examples:
- 7 Headline Mistakes Costing You Clicks
- The #1 Error Killing Your Conversion Rates
- 5 Content Mistakes That Tank Your SEO

**When to use**: Problem-aware audience, cautionary content

---

### 8. Comparison
**Formula**: [Option A] vs [Option B]: [Which/What/How]

Examples:
- WordPress vs Webflow: Which Is Right for You?
- Long-Form vs Short-Form: What Performs Better?
- Content Quality vs Quantity: The Real Answer

**When to use**: Comparison content, decision-stage readers

---

### 9. Newsjacking/Timely
**Formula**: [Topic/Event] + [Your Take/Insight]

Examples:
- What Google's Latest Update Means for Your Content
- 2024 Content Trends: What's Actually Working
- The New Instagram Algorithm: How to Adapt

**When to use**: Trending topics, news, updates

---

### 10. Orbit Formula
**Formula**: [Target Keyphrase]: [Number] [Trigger Word] [Promise]

Examples:
- Email Marketing: 7 Strategies That Actually Convert
- Content Repurposing: The Complete System for 10x Output
- Blog Writing: 5 Secrets to Posts That Rank

## Power Words by Category

### Curiosity
Secret, Hidden, Surprising, Unusual, Strange, Weird, Unknown, Insider, Behind-the-scenes

### Urgency
Now, Today, Quick, Fast, Instant, Limited, Deadline, Before, While

### Value
Free, Bonus, Exclusive, Premium, Ultimate, Complete, Proven, Guaranteed

### Emotion
Amazing, Incredible, Powerful, Inspiring, Heartbreaking, Shocking

### Authority
Expert, Research, Study, Science, Data, Proven, Official

### Transformation
Transform, Revolution, Breakthrough, Game-changing, Life-changing

## Headline Testing

### A/B Test Elements
- Number vs no number
- Question vs statement
- Benefit vs curiosity
- Short vs long
- With vs without power words

### Scoring Criteria

Rate each headline 1-10 on:

| Criterion | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| Clarity | Is it immediately understandable? |
| Specificity | Does it make a concrete promise? |
| Emotion | Does it create an emotional response? |
| Curiosity | Does it make you want to know more? |
| Benefit | Is the value clear? |
| Uniqueness | Does it stand out from competitors? |

**Minimum score to publish**: 35/60

---

## Headline Checklist

Before finalizing:

- [ ] At least 25 variations written
- [ ] Top 3 scored against criteria
- [ ] 50-60 characters or less
- [ ] Primary keyword included
- [ ] No clickbait (content delivers on promise)
- [ ] Active voice used
- [ ] Specific benefit or curiosity clear
- [ ] Would you click this in search results?
- [ ] Does it work without context?
- [ ] Tested with someone unfamiliar with topic

---

## Headlines to Avoid

**Clickbait patterns:**
- "You Won't Believe..."
- "This Will Shock You"
- "[X] HATES This One Trick"
- Excessive caps or punctuation!!!
- Vague promises with no specificity

**Weak patterns:**
- Starting with "I" or "My"
- "Introduction to [Topic]"
- "Everything You Need to Know"
- "[Topic] 101"
- Generic descriptions

**Accuracy issues:**
- Promising more than content delivers
- Using "best" without proof
- Claims you can't back up
- Misleading angles
