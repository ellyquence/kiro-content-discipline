# Content Structure Frameworks

## Choosing a Framework

Select based on content goal and audience state:

| Framework | Best For | Reader State |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| AIDA | Awareness, conversions | Cold, curious |
| PAS | Problem-solving, sales | Frustrated, seeking solution |
| BAB | Transformation stories | Aspirational, dreaming |
| 4Ps | Bottom-funnel, decisions | Comparing, evaluating |
| Story Arc | Engagement, brand building | Open, entertainment-seeking |
| How-To | Education, tutorials | Learning, task-focused |

---

## AIDA Framework

**Attention → Interest → Desire → Action**

Best for: Landing pages, promotional content, awareness-stage blog posts

### Structure

**1. Attention (5-10% of content)**
- Bold claim or surprising statistic
- Challenge a common belief
- Ask a provocative question
- Lead with the outcome

**2. Interest (25-30% of content)**
- Expand on the hook with details
- Share relevant data and trends
- Tell a relatable story
- Build curiosity about the solution

**3. Desire (40-50% of content)**
- Present your solution/main content
- Show benefits (not just features)
- Include social proof
- Paint the "after" picture
- Address objections

**4. Action (10-15% of content)**
- Clear, specific CTA
- Create urgency if appropriate
- Reduce friction
- Provide next steps

### AIDA Example Outline

```markdown
# [Attention-Grabbing Headline with Benefit]

[Opening hook: Surprising stat or bold claim]

## Why This Matters Now [Interest]
- Current problem/trend
- Stakes of not acting
- Building curiosity

## The Solution: [Main Topic] [Desire]

### Benefit 1
[Explanation + proof]

### Benefit 2
[Explanation + proof]

### Benefit 3
[Explanation + proof]

### What Others Are Saying
[Social proof / testimonials]

## Get Started Today [Action]
[Clear CTA with next steps]
```

## PAS Framework

**Problem → Agitation → Solution**

Best for: Sales pages, problem-aware audiences, pain-point content

### Structure

**1. Problem (15-20% of content)**
- Name the specific problem clearly
- Show you understand their situation
- Use their language
- Make them feel seen

**2. Agitation (25-35% of content)**
- Dig into the pain
- Explore consequences of inaction
- Describe the frustration
- Amplify the urgency
- "What happens if this continues?"

**3. Solution (45-55% of content)**
- Present your answer
- Explain how it works
- Prove it works (evidence)
- Show the transformation
- Make it accessible

### PAS Example Outline

```markdown
# [Headline Naming the Problem]

## The Problem You're Facing
[Describe the pain point in detail]

You've probably tried:
- [Failed solution 1]
- [Failed solution 2]
- [Failed solution 3]

And nothing works.

## Why This Keeps Getting Worse
[Agitation - consequences, frustrations]

### The Hidden Cost
[What they're losing: time, money, opportunity]

### What Happens If You Don't Fix This
[Future pain if unchanged]

## There's a Better Way
[Introduce solution]

### How It Works
[Explain the solution]

### Why It's Different
[Differentiation]

### Real Results
[Proof and examples]

## Your Next Step
[CTA]
```

## BAB Framework

**Before → After → Bridge**

Best for: Transformation stories, aspirational content, case studies

### Structure

**1. Before (20-25%)**
- Paint the current painful state
- Specific, relatable details
- "This is where you are"

**2. After (25-30%)**
- Vivid picture of the desired state
- Specific outcomes and feelings
- "This is where you could be"

**3. Bridge (45-50%)**
- How to get from Before to After
- Your solution/content as the path
- Step-by-step guidance

### BAB Example Outline

```markdown
# [Transformation-Focused Headline]

## Where You Are Now
[Paint the "before" picture]
- Current struggles
- Daily frustrations
- What's not working

## Where You Could Be
[Paint the "after" picture]
- Desired outcomes
- How life/work changes
- The feelings of success

## How to Get There
[The bridge - your main content]

### Step 1: [First Action]
### Step 2: [Second Action]
### Step 3: [Third Action]

## Start Your Transformation
[CTA]
```

## 4Ps Framework

**Promise → Picture → Proof → Push**

Best for: Bottom-funnel content, comparison pages, decision-stage content

### Structure

**1. Promise (10-15%)**
- Make a specific, compelling promise
- What will they gain?
- Be bold but believable

**2. Picture (20-25%)**
- Help them visualize the outcome
- Sensory details
- "Imagine when..."

**3. Proof (40-50%)**
- Evidence your promise is real
- Case studies
- Testimonials
- Data and results
- Credentials

**4. Push (15-20%)**
- Compelling CTA
- Urgency or scarcity (if genuine)
- Risk reversal (guarantees)
- Clear next steps

## Story Arc Framework

**Hook → Context → Rising Action → Climax → Resolution → Takeaway**

Best for: Thought leadership, brand storytelling, engagement-focused content

### Structure

**1. Hook**
- Open in the middle of action
- Start with dialogue or tension
- Create immediate curiosity

**2. Context**
- Set the scene
- Introduce the characters/situation
- Establish stakes

**3. Rising Action**
- Build tension
- Introduce challenges
- Show the struggle

**4. Climax**
- The turning point
- Key decision or discovery
- Peak emotional moment

**5. Resolution**
- What changed
- The outcome
- New equilibrium

**6. Takeaway**
- What the reader learns
- Applicable insight
- Connection to their situation

## How-To Framework

**Overview → Steps → Troubleshooting → Next Level**

Best for: Tutorials, guides, educational content

### Structure

```markdown
# How to [Achieve Outcome]

## Overview
- What we'll cover
- What you'll be able to do
- Time/skill required
- What you need

## Step 1: [First Action]
[Detailed instructions]
[Screenshot/example if applicable]

## Step 2: [Second Action]
[Detailed instructions]
[Screenshot/example if applicable]

## Step 3: [Third Action]
[Detailed instructions]
[Screenshot/example if applicable]

[Continue as needed]

## Common Issues & Fixes
- Problem: [Issue]
  Solution: [Fix]

## Taking It Further
[Advanced tips or next steps]

## Summary
[Quick recap of key steps]
```

## Framework Selection Checklist

Ask these questions to choose:

1. **What's the reader's awareness level?**
   - Unaware of problem → AIDA
   - Problem-aware → PAS
   - Solution-aware → 4Ps

2. **What's the content goal?**
   - Build awareness → AIDA, Story Arc
   - Drive action → PAS, 4Ps
   - Educate → How-To
   - Inspire → BAB, Story Arc

3. **What's the emotional state?**
   - Frustrated → PAS
   - Curious → AIDA
   - Aspirational → BAB
   - Analytical → How-To, 4Ps
